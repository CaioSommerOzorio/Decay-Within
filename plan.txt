[Game Mechanics]
=====================
Players get 1 free draw per turn and 1 free summon per turn
For every extra summon, players pay x vitality, and for every extra draw
players get 1 decay counter.
The game is won when the opponent's core is destroyed, either through attacking or through decay counters
Players start with 50 vitality each.
Different formats may start with different vitality.
Players start with 50 life each

[Combat]
=============
Each turn after moving, there is a combat step.
If there are one or more creatures in the same zone as an opponent's creature, then combat begins on that zone.
The active player chooses the order of combat for the zones.
The players each pick up their creatures from the zone, and start placing them face down separated into 3 lanes one after the other, starting with the non active player.

[Summoning]
There are 4 ranks that each cost vitality to play:
Soldier : 2 vitality
Sergeant: 5 vitality 
Captain : 8 vitality 
General : 12 vitality

[Motherships]
==================
Each mothership defines what tribes are allowed to contain in your deck
Motherships start in play
Each mothership has a core, energy and control sector.
Players can defeat their opponent's sectors to weaken their buffs.
For example: a mothership may have:
	Core: buff 1
	Energy: buff 2
	Control: buff 3
If the energy sector is destroyed, the energy buff is taken away.
If the core dies, the player loses.

[Tribes]
=============
Arachnids - Time Weavers
	Theme: Delaying summons
	Subtheme: Summoning outside of turn
	Mechanics: 
		- Threadmark: casting spells on an earlier turn for a payoff on a later turn
		- Echo: Threadmarked spells can be copied
	Flavor: Patient, waiting for the right moment

Specters - Echo Raiders
	Theme: Direct damage and burn
	Subtheme: Death triggers
	Mechanics: 
		- Aftermath: Once a specter dies it deals more damage
		- Phasing: Slipping through opponent's defences to directly damage their ship
	Flavor: Short burts of direct damage

Swarmkin - Endless Replicators
	Theme: Flooding the field
	Subtheme: Merging creatures
	Mechanics:
		- Overrun: Benefits from having multiple of one 
		- Mitosis: Create copies when it dies
	Flavor: Easy to wipe but must wipe all, otherwise they multiply.

Parasitic Bots - Host Enhancers 
	Theme: Can be inserted into any deck
	Subtheme: Gaining control of creatures
	Mechanics:
		- Attach: Can attach upgrades to other cards
		- Hijack: Can attach to an enemy system to weaken it
	Flavor: Powerless by itself but with the right host, can be powerful

Fungi - Biomass Collective
	Theme: Decreasing decay/vitality gain
	Subtheme: High defence low attack
	Mechanics:
		- Regenerate: Remove one or more decay counters from you
		- Bloom: Payoff for removing decay counters
	Flavor: Can work as either vitality gain or enable more card/spells to be drawn/cast

Wraith - Shadow Corrupts
	Theme: More decay means stronger troops
	Subtheme: Decay counter delay (temporary immunity from decay damage)
	Mechanics:
		- Corruption: Abilities/stats increase the more decay counters you have
		- Haunt: Weaken an enemy system after death
	Flavor: Works with decay counters and edging death

Eyes - All Knower and All Seer
	Theme: Can look at opponent's hand/library
	Subtheme: Playing cards from places other than your hand
	Mechanics: 
		- Clairvoyance: Peek at opponent’s hand or top of library each turn.
		- Premonition: Payoff once opponents play a card of the chosen type
	Flavor: Know all outcomes, can make decisions based on information

Doppelgangers - Deceiving Shapeshifters
	Theme: Own creatures and playstyle depend on the opponent's
	Subtheme: Group hug: more creatures from opponents means more creatures for you
	Mechanics:
		- Mutate: Cards can change characteristics after they're in play
		- Mime: Enter the field as a copy of an opponent’s card but with a twist.
	Flavor: What you do, I do better

Demons - Soul Fetchers
	Theme: Reanimating cards
	Subtheme: Discarding and enter the graveyard triggers
	Mechanics:
		- Soul fetch: Creature comes back for a turn
		- Feed the infernum: Make opponent's discard
	Flavor: Working with the graveyard 

[Lore]
The Arachnids once wove the fabric of time.
Every choice, every battle, every future was caught in their web.

But the Wraiths broke the weave.

Hungering for more power, they tore at the threads,
feeding on the edges of death until the strands unraveled.
From their greed, Decay was born —
a creeping force that corrodes every action and choice.

The Arachnids tried to contain it,
to weave new patterns that could hold the rot.
But the other tribes saw opportunity.

The Specters turned Decay into fire,
striking fast and burning bright.

The Swarmkin multiplied through it,
each loss replaced by ten more.

The Fungi grew from it,
converting rot into fresh strength.

The Eyes watched futures split apart,
they whispered of what would happen.
But never of what should.

The Doppelgangers mirrored what they saw,
becoming whatever the war demanded.

Now the Motherships awaken.
Their cores flicker, their sectors weaken,
and the tribes gather for war on the shared world below
